Ingredients & Chemical Science

Thymolphthalein (1 gram)

This is the heart of the disappearing ink. It is a pH indicator that remains colorless in neutral or acidic conditions but turns into a rich, deep blue color when the pH rises above 9.3. When the pH drops again (due to air or acid), it becomes colorless once more — creating the magical disappearing effect.

Ethyl Alcohol (100 mL)

Used as the primary solvent because thymolphthalein does not dissolve easily in water. It ensures complete dissolution and helps the ink dry quickly when applied on paper or fabric.

Distilled Water (900 mL)

Dilutes the concentrated solution to make approximately 1 liter of usable ink. Distilled water is essential to prevent any impurities from interfering with the delicate pH balance.

Sodium Hydroxide 3M (10 mL)

A strong base that raises the pH of the solution to around 10–11, triggering the blue color. It must be added carefully with proper safety precautions as it is caustic in concentrated form.

Step-by-Step Preparation Process

01

Dissolve 1 gram of thymolphthalein in 100 mL ethyl alcohol.

Stir continuously until the solution becomes completely clear.

02

Add 900 mL of distilled water slowly while stirring.

The mixture may appear milky white at this stage. This is normal.

03

Carefully add 10 mL of 3M Sodium Hydroxide.

Add drop by drop while stirring. The solution will instantly turn deep blue.

04

Transfer to a sealed container.

Your disappearing ink is now ready for use and demonstration.

Quality Control

Every batch is carefully tested to ensure the pH remains between 10–11, the blue color is vibrant, and the ink disappears within 15 seconds when exposed to breath (CO₂) or mild acid like vinegar.

Storage

Store the ink in a cool, dark place inside an amber or dark bottle. When properly sealed, the solution remains stable and effective for more than 12 months.
